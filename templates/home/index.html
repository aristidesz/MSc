About the Model

SGLOBE-rani Model is a new global whole-mantle model of isotropic and radially anisotropic S-velocity structure. It is based on approximately 43,000,000 surface-wave and 420,000 body-wave travel time measurements which are expanded in spherical harmonics basis functions up to degree 35. The model takes into account short-period group velocity data with period range at 16 seconds. Thus, these data are strongly sensitive to the crust perturbations which are incorporated in the model. The isotropic S-velocity of the model are in a high agreement with previous upper mantle models. The anisotropic model is in an agreement with previous models; however; it shows some deviations for faster SV velocity along subduction zones at transition depths and faster SH velocity beneath slabs in the lower mantle.

The main differences from previous studies for isotropic and radially anisotropic whole-mantle model are that: SGLOBE-rani use a larger dataset of body-wave travel times and surface wave dispersion measurements including short-period group velocity data and secondly, it inverts jointly for isotropic, anisotropic and crustal thickness perturbations as model parameters.

Reference

Chang, S.-J., A. M. G. Ferreira, J. Ritsema, H. J.van Heijst, and J. H. Woodhouse (2015), Joint inversion for global isotropic and radially anisotropic mantle structure including crustal thickness perturbations. J. Geophys. Res. Solid Earth, 120, 4278–4300. doi: 10.1002/2014JB011824.
